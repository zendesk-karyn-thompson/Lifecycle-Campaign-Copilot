The Basics: What is Nurture?

Before we dive into our specific program, let's establish the fundamentals of what nurture is and why it matters.

What is a Nurture Program?

A nurture program is an always-on, automated email series designed to maintain relationships with leads who aren't yet ready to buy. Think of it as the connective tissue between your big campaigns—it keeps leads warm, engaged, and progressing through their buyer journey when you're not actively promoting something.

Why Do We Have Nurture Programs?

Most leads aren't ready to buy when they first hear from you. In fact:

  • Only 3-5% of your market is actively buying at any given time
  • The average B2B sales cycle is 6-18 months, depending on complexity
  • Buyers consume 10+ pieces of content before making a decision

Without nurture, leads go cold during the inevitable gaps between your campaigns. Nurture bridges these gaps, keeping your brand top-of-mind and helping leads progress toward readiness.

What Nurture IS

  • An always-on, year-round program
  • Behavior-driven content delivery
  • A supporting player to your campaigns
  • Focused on engagement, not conversion
  • About quality touchpoints over time

What Nurture is NOT

  • A campaign replacement
  • A direct lead-gen machine
  • A one-size-fits-all blast
  • Measured primarily by MQLs
  • Designed to push immediate action

The Problem We're Solving

Without nurture, leads experience a "feast or famine" communication pattern. They get hit with your campaigns, then hear nothing for weeks or months. This creates disengagement, lost momentum, and colder leads. Pre-MQL nurture provides consistent, valuable touchpoints that keep leads engaged year-round.

The Goals: What Success Looks Like

Nurture success is NOT about MQLs. It's about creating sustained engagement that signals genuine interest.

Primary Success Metric

👁️ Engaged Web Sessions

This is our North Star. An engaged web session means a lead visited your site and took meaningful actions—viewing multiple pages, exploring products, or reading resources. This shows real intent, not just a random click.

Why We Measure Engaged Sessions, Not MQLs

Here's the critical distinction:

  • Nurture is not a conversion program—it's an engagement program
  • MQLs are primarily driven by campaigns (webinars, events, high-intent content)
  • Nurture's job is to keep leads engaged so they're warmer when campaigns do hit
  • Engaged web sessions are leading indicators of future conversion
  • You can't directly attribute MQLs to nurture because multiple touches influence conversion

Think of it this way: Nurture is like tending a garden. You're watering, fertilizing, and maintaining the soil. The harvest (MQLs) comes from campaigns, but the garden only produces because of the nurture work.

Segmentation Criteria

Leads enter nurture streams based on:

  • Lead source: How they came to us (content syndication, web form, event, etc.)
  • Declared interests: What they told us they care about
  • Behavioral signals: Pages visited, content consumed, past engagement
  • Lead temperature: Cold (content syndi) vs. warm (trial, demo request)
  • Company fit: ICP match, company size, industry

Behaviors We're Looking For

Success in nurture is driven by two key behaviors that signal genuine interest and engagement.

📧 Email Engagement

When leads open and click nurture emails, it signals initial interest. We track:

  • Opens (directional, not scored)
  • Clicks on content links
  • Multiple clicks in one email
  • Engagement patterns over time

🌐 Website Content Consumption

This is where the real magic happens. We're looking for:

  • Engaged web sessions: 2+ pages, 1+ minute on site
  • Viewing product/solution pages
  • Reading blog posts, resources, case studies
  • Exploring pricing or trial pages
  • Repeat visits over time

🔍 Progressive Engagement

We're watching for signals that indicate warming interest:

  • Increasing email engagement over time
  • Moving from generic to specific content
  • Direct website visits (not just email clicks)
  • Interacting with multiple content types

⚡ High-Intent Actions

These behaviors signal someone is getting closer to conversion:

  • Downloading multiple assets
  • Attending webinars or events
  • Starting a free trial
  • Requesting a demo
  • Engaging with sales content

The Engagement Flywheel

Nurture creates a virtuous cycle: Email → Click → Website Visit → Engaged Session → Higher Lead Score → More Relevant Content → Repeat. Each behavior informs the next, creating a personalized journey.

What to Expect: Lead Journey

When a lead enters Pre-MQL nurture, here's what happens and what you can expect.

Entry & Onboarding

Week 1: New leads receive a welcome email introducing your brand and value proposition. This sets expectations and establishes the relationship.

Ongoing Cadence

Weeks 2-30: Nurture programs run anywhere from 7-30 weeks long, depending on persona and engagement. Leads receive persona-specific emails approximately every 1-2 weeks. The content adapts based on their behavior—what they click, what they read, what they ignore.

  • High velocity nurture: Weekly emails for warm leads
  • Standard nurture: Bi-weekly emails for most leads
  • Reduced frequency: Monthly for cold or disengaged leads

Content Progression: Aware → Inform → Engage

Content evolves as leads engage, following our three-stage framework:

  • Aware (Early stage): Educational, problem-focused content (blogs, guides, industry insights). Goal: Build awareness and credibility.
  • Inform (Mid stage): Solution-focused content (case studies, webinars, how-to guides). Goal: Educate on solutions and approaches.
  • Engage (Late stage): Product-focused content (demos, trials, pricing, customer stories). Goal: Drive consideration and action.

Integration with Campaigns

Nurture doesn't run in isolation. When you launch a campaign, nurture either:

  • Pauses for leads receiving your campaign (no double-tapping)
  • Continues alongside for leads not in your campaign audience
  • Resumes afterward for leads who didn't convert

Exit Conditions

Leads exit nurture when they:

  • Reach MQL status (passed to sales)
  • Start a trial (enter product-led nurture)
  • Request a demo (enter sales-led journey)
  • Become completely disengaged (moved to re-engagement campaign)

Best Practices & Realistic Expectations

Set yourself up for success by understanding what works—and what doesn't—in nurture programs.

✅ Best Practices

  • Quality over quantity: Send less email per person, reach more unique people
  • Behavior-driven content: Let actions (not assumptions) determine what they see next
  • Always-on mindset: Nurture never stops, even during campaign season
  • Measure engagement, not just opens: Focus on engaged sessions and meaningful actions
  • Give it time: Nurture is a long game. 6-12 months to see full impact
  • Integrate with campaigns: Coordinate timing to avoid inbox fatigue

❌ Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Expecting immediate MQLs: Nurture is not a conversion engine
  • Overloading with emails: More is not better. Respect attention
  • One-size-fits-all content: Personalization matters. Segment meaningfully
  • Ignoring disengagement: If they're not opening, reduce frequency or pause
  • Treating it like a campaign: Nurture is ongoing, not time-bound
  • Not giving it time: Shutting down nurture after 2 months is too early

Setting Realistic Expectations for Stakeholders

When explaining nurture to leadership or campaign owners, be clear about timelines and outcomes:

  • Short-term (0-3 months): Expect baseline engagement. Opens, clicks, some web sessions. Don't expect MQL spikes.
  • Mid-term (3-6 months): Engaged sessions increase. You'll see leads warming up and responding better to campaigns.
  • Long-term (6-12+ months): Pipeline impact becomes visible. Nurtured leads convert faster and at higher rates when they do engage with campaigns.

The bottom line: Nurture is infrastructure, not a silver bullet. It makes everything else work better over time.
